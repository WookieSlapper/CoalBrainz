WELCOME

Hello! Welcome to the web site of WookieSlapper, a shiny-new programmer charting his way through a mid-life career change.

IN THE BEGINNING

So how does one become WookieSlapper? And what makes someone decide to be a forty-something entry-level programmer? Well, my friends, just sit right back, and you'll hear a tale...


It all started when education became a business. I was a tenured high school English teacher in a small rural school in New York State. In the same year the bean-counters there gave our district Superintendent (who had yet to be hired) a $20,000 raise, the decision was made to eliminate over a dozen teaching positions due to a lack of budgetary resources (pay attention to the irony here!). This meant that all the work I had done to become a teacher, including acquiring a Master's degree in English and Education, and all of the 10 years I had spent as a teacher, instantly became worthless. I then set myself adrift in a sea of doubt and regret. I was 41, with a mortgage and two children, and no job or career prospects. I'm also pretty sure I was being stalked by a white whale . . . .

AND THEN THERE WAS LIGHT, AND IT WAS GOOD

So, I have this friend, Spike. One day, my friend says to me, "Why don't you try computer programming?" And then I says back to him, I says, "What is an old guy like me going to do, go back to school and start all over again? I'm too old for that."

He then went on to tell me about all the glories of learning to code, how anyone could learn it. He told me about how there was a dearth of programmers, and that there were always going to be jobs. This was, after all, the dawn of a new digital age. He went on to tell me about people who were in the same position I was who found new life and new hope in front of a keyboard, and how he knew of a middle-aged woman who left her career to start a life as a programmer. I then got invited to this party he threw that was filled with computer geeks (I'm pretty sure it was a set-up). Within a couple of weeks, I was already working on CodeAcademy, learning how to write code in HTML, CSS, Javascript, and JQuery. I was hooked.

IN THE LION'S DEN

And so, now we come to it. I created this web site as a first official "project" to test out all of the things I've been learning, and continue to learn. This web site is a work in progress, so please forgive me if there are mistakes along the way. What you see before you is a result of time spent learning Visual Studio, SourceTree, GitHub, HTML, CSS, Javascript, C#, and a bunch of other libraries, frameworks, and extensions, not to mention the logic and syntax that goes along with it. It's a liquid thing, and it will flow and change with time, hopefully into something awesome and worthwhile.

If you're interested in keeping up with how things go, please check out the Blog and Resume pages occassionally, which will be updated with progress, problems, pains and parables (and not without a little alliteration and humor). I look forward to sharing the journey with you, and hearing back from you on how you think I'm doing. See you on the other side!
